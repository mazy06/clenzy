# Service requests

## What is the difference between a service request and an intervention?

The service request expresses a need: "this property needs a full cleaning for this date", "there is a leak to repair". It comes before the intervention and frames the job: service type, property involved, desired date, priority, price estimate or quote. The intervention is the concrete execution in the field: a mission scheduled and assigned to a team or a provider, with photos, checklist and closure. In Baitly, a service request automatically becomes an intervention once a team is found and the payment for the service is confirmed: you do not have to create the intervention by hand. Requests are managed in the Interventions module, Service Requests tab (title "Service Requests", subtitle "Manage maintenance and cleaning requests").

## How do I create a service request?

In Interventions, Service Requests tab, click "New request". The form unfolds in sections: basic information (a short, meaningful title), description, affected property, priority and planning, requester and assignment. First choose the nature of the need among three families: Cleaning, Maintenance / Works, or Other (with the option to enter a reusable custom type). For a cleaning, compose the service a la carte: linen, ironing, deep kitchen cleaning, disinfection, windows, bay windows, exterior, and so on. For maintenance, a priced catalog by domain (plumbing, electricity...) feeds a line-by-line quote. Complete the form with the priority (Low, Normal, High, Critical), whether it is urgent, the desired due date, the preferred time slot, and if needed special instructions and property access notes; the request can be linked to a reservation. Finish with "Create request".

## What are the statuses of a request and what do they mean?

A request follows a status cycle visible in the list and on its record. "Pending": the request has just been created, and Baitly is looking for an available team or provider. "Awaiting payment": a team has been found (automatically or assigned by hand) and the service is waiting for payment. "In progress": the payment is confirmed and the service is launched (the corresponding intervention is created). "Completed": the service is finished. "Cancelled": the request was abandoned. "Rejected": the request was not accepted; it can be put back to pending to re-enter the flow. The request list can be filtered by status, type and priority, with a search by title, description or property name, and several display modes (table, grid, geographic map).

## How is a request assigned and then turned into an intervention?

Upon creation, Baitly attempts an automatic assignment: the platform looks for a team available for the requested property and date (if automatic assignment is enabled in your workflow settings). If a team is found, the request moves to "Awaiting payment". Otherwise, the search is retried regularly, then an "Action required: manual assignment" alert is raised to managers; the "Assign" button then lets you choose the team yourself. If the assigned team declines the mission, the request goes back to "Pending" and a new search starts automatically (with, for cleaning, a provider replacement suggestion). Once the payment for the service is confirmed, the intervention is created automatically from the request, carrying over its title, description, type and linked reservation: you will find it in the Interventions module, ready to be tracked in the field.

## Who can create and manage service requests?

Creating a request is open to managers: the owner (host) can create and view their requests; platform administrators and managers can additionally edit them, assign them, change their status or delete them. Field teams (technicians, housekeepers, supervisors) do not create requests: they receive the interventions that result from them and handle them from the mobile app or the web. An issue reported by an agent in the field (damage noticed during a cleaning, for example) can, however, be converted into a service request by a manager, which restarts the full flow. Guests do not create service requests either: to offer them paid services during their stay, use the paid services of the welcome guide instead.

## How do I track the price: estimate, recommended price and quote?

Every request carries a financial dimension. For cleaning, Baitly computes a recommended price from the property's characteristics and the chosen services; the request's estimate can differ from it, with the gap displayed for information. For maintenance, two modes exist: the direct quote, built line by line from the priced catalog (label, quantity, unit price), or the diagnostic mode, where a diagnostic fee is billed first and the full quote is drawn up after the technician's visit. The request's record summarizes the estimated cost and, once the service is done, the actual cost. Payment for the service happens at the "Awaiting payment" step, before the intervention is launched; the request's comments allow you to discuss the need and the pricing.

## Frequently asked questions

**Do I have to create the intervention myself after validating a request?**
No. As soon as the team is found and the payment confirmed, the intervention is created automatically from the request, with the same information.

**Why does my request stay "Pending"?**
No available team has been found yet for the property and date. Baitly retries automatically; you can also assign a team manually with the "Assign" button.

**Can a technician create a service request?**
No, field roles do not create requests. They handle the interventions that result from them, and can report an issue that a manager will convert into a request.

**What happens if the provider declines the mission?**
The request goes back to "Pending" and a new team search starts automatically. For a cleaning, a provider replacement suggestion is offered to managers.
