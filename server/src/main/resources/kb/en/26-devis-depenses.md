# Quotes and expenses in Baitly

## How do I draw up a quote for a maintenance intervention?

In Baitly, the quote is built when pricing a maintenance service request (Interventions page, Service Requests tab). Two pricing modes exist. The detailed quote: you add lines made of a label, a quantity and a unit price; shortcuts offer the most common labels ("Labor", "Parts / materials", "Travel"); each line's total (quantity times unit price) and the grand total are calculated automatically, and this total feeds the request's estimated cost. The other mode is the diagnostic fee: when the problem is not yet identified, you bill only a diagnostic charge; the detailed quote can be drawn up afterwards, once the diagnosis is made. Amounts are entered in the reference currency of your rates.

## How do I track the acceptance and status of a quote?

The quote follows the life cycle of the service request it is attached to. The statuses run as follows: Pending (request created), Approved (validated by a manager), Quote accepted (the client accepted the pricing), Awaiting payment (if payment is required before the intervention), then In progress and Completed once the intervention is carried out; a request can also be Cancelled or Rejected. The request's detail includes an "Approval and quote" section that records who approved the request and who accepted the quote ("Estimate accepted by"). You are notified at the key steps (request approved, awaiting payment, and so on) through the notification center. Once the quote is accepted and the payment made if required, the request becomes an intervention that can be scheduled and assigned.

## What is the cleaning quote sent to the owner?

For cleaning, Baitly offers a quote of a particular kind: the cleaning quote. From a property's cleaning pricing, the "Cleaning quote" button generates a PDF document summarizing the recommended price per service type, the price range and the estimated duration, calculated from the property's characteristics (surface area, bedrooms, floors, exterior, linen, number of guests...). After confirmation, this document is emailed to the property's owner. It is an informational document, with no signature: it is meant to align the cleaning rate with the owner, not to commit a payment. For how the recommended cleaning price is calculated in full, see the pricing article.

## How do I record an expense in Baitly?

Expenses are managed in the Billing menu, Expenses tab (access restricted to platform managers). This screen tracks provider expenses (cleaning, maintenance, laundry...) related to your properties. The "New expense" button opens a form where you enter: the provider, the property involved, a description, the amount excluding tax, the VAT rate (the amount including tax is calculated), the category, the date, an optional invoice reference and notes. You can attach a receipt (PDF or photo). The available categories are: Cleaning, Maintenance, Laundry, Supplies, Landscaping and Other. Counters at the top of the screen give an overview: total expenses, pending expenses, approved expenses.

## How do I track the status of an expense?

Each expense goes through clear statuses: Draft (entered, awaiting validation), Approved (validated by a manager, with a receipt attached if needed), Included in payout (integrated into the calculation of an owner payout), Paid and Cancelled. The actions available on an expense are: Approve, Mark as paid (with a payment reference), Cancel, and generating a Purchase order. A status filter lets you isolate, for example, all draft expenses awaiting validation. The general principle in three steps: create the expense, approve it, then let it be deducted automatically from payouts, as explained in the next section.

## What is the link between expenses and the profitability of my properties?

Approved expenses are automatically deducted from owner payouts: in the Billing menu, Payouts tab, each payout shows an Expenses column and its detail shows the amount deducted line by line. The expense then moves to the "Included in payout" status. This mechanism gives a faithful view of profitability per property: stay revenue on one side, operating costs (cleaning, maintenance, supplies...) on the other, reconciled property by property since each expense is attached to a property and a category. For the overall analysis (margins, fiscal reports, accounting exports), see the Billing menu, Reports & Exports tab, and the article dedicated to analytics and reports.

## Frequently asked questions

**Where do you create a quote in Baitly?**
The detailed quote (lines with label / quantity / unit price) is created when pricing a maintenance service request, on the Interventions page. The cleaning quote, on the other hand, is an informational PDF generated from the cleaning pricing and sent to the owner.

**What does the "Quote accepted" status mean on a request?**
The pricing has been accepted; the request then moves to awaiting payment if payment is required, and afterwards becomes an intervention to schedule.

**Who can see and manage the Expenses tab?**
The Expenses tab of the Billing menu is restricted to platform managers. Owners see the effect of expenses in the detail of their payouts.

**Is an expense billed back to the owner?**
An approved expense is automatically deducted from the payout of the owner concerned; the payout's detail keeps an exact record of it.

**Can I attach a supplier invoice to an expense?**
Yes: at approval time, you can attach a receipt (PDF or photo) and enter the invoice reference.
