# Digital welcome guide and guest experience

## What is the Baitly digital welcome guide?

The digital welcome guide is an elegant web page your guests browse before and during their stay: welcome message, wifi, access code, house rules, amenities, arrival guide, recommendations around the property and add-on services. It is managed from the Booking & welcome menu, Welcome guide tab. Each guide is attached to a property and linked to a reservation: the guest accesses it through a secure link specific to their reservation, whose validity is limited to the stay. The wifi, door code, rules and useful numbers are filled in automatically from the property's detail page — you only add the welcome message and your recommendations. Roles involved: hosts and managers create and publish the guides; the guest views it, with no account to create.

## How do I create, customize and publish a welcome guide?

In Booking & welcome, Welcome guide tab, click "New guide", choose the property (it must have a current or upcoming reservation) and compose the content: a title, a welcome note with your signature (the guest's first name is displayed automatically, loaded from the reservation), carousel cover photos chosen from the property's photos, and free-form sections (text, accordion steps for a step-by-step arrival guide, a rules list, a list with icons). On the appearance side, seven themes are offered (Atelier, Noir, Jardin, Azur, Corail, Brume, Minuit) plus a custom color and logo. A real-time guest preview is displayed while editing. Each guide has a language (French, English or Arabic); to serve several languages, create one guide per language. Finally, publish the guide from the list (Draft or Published status): only a published guide is accessible to guests. The share link and a downloadable QR code are available on each guide.

## What does the guide contain on the guest side (recommendations, activities, guestbook, chatbot)?

Beyond practical information, the guide offers blocks that can be enabled individually. The "around me" recommendations: a map of points of interest (restaurants, beaches, markets...) added by the host with a personal note. Activities: experiences to book, selected by the host. The guestbook: guests leave a note and a rating, visible to the host. The assistant chatbot: an AI answers the guest's questions based on the guide's and property's content (wifi, parking, check-out...). Paid services are added to these (see the dedicated section). Each block can be checked or unchecked in the guide's settings. The host also has viewing statistics (guide openings, clicks on activities, chatbot messages, etc.) to measure what their guests use. If the property is equipped with a smart lock, door unlocking can be offered from the guide.

## How does the arrival form (guest declaration) work?

Before accessing the guide, the guest can be asked to complete a short arrival declaration form, which fulfills the legal guest declaration obligation (police record): the main guest's identity (last name, first name, date and place of birth, nationality, residential address, ID document type and number — passport, identity card or residence permit) and that of their travel companions, added one by one. The data is transmitted securely to the host and kept for the legal retention period. The guest can also indicate their estimated arrival time and special requests as part of online check-in. Once the form is validated, they access the full guide. Roles involved: the guest fills it in; the host and managers view the collected information to prepare the arrival.

## How do I sell add-on services to guests (upsells)?

The Paid services tab of the Booking & welcome menu lets you offer add-on services to your guests: early check-in, late check-out, extra cleaning, transfer, welcome basket, etc. Each service has a category, a title, a price, a currency, an image and can be attached to a specific property or to all properties. Suggestions of typical services help you get started. On each guide, you choose which services are displayed (unchecking a service hides it on that guide only). The guest pays directly from the guide by card; your share is paid out through your usual payments, with the platform taking a commission. Sales and commission tracking is available in the same tab. Roles involved: hosts and managers configure; the guest purchases from their guide.

## Frequently asked questions

**Does the guest need to create an account to see the guide?**
No. They simply open the secure link (or scan the QR code) linked to their reservation; access is limited to the duration of the stay.

**Why can't I create a guide for a property?**
A guide is linked to a reservation: if there is no current or upcoming reservation for this property, creation is not possible. If a guide already exists for the reservation, Baitly offers to overwrite it.

**Is the guide translated automatically?**
No. Each guide has one language (French, English or Arabic); to cover several languages, create one guide per language.

**Do I have to enter the wifi and door code by hand?**
No, they are picked up automatically from the property's detail page (check-in instructions). Update the detail page and the guide follows.

**Can I see what guests view in the guide?**
Yes: each guide has its statistics (openings, clicks on activities, chatbot messages, check-in), accessible from the guide list.
