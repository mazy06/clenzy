# The Baitly notification center

## Where do I find my notifications in Baitly?

The notification center is reached from the bell icon in the sidebar of the web application: a badge shows the number of unread notifications, and one click opens the Notifications page. This page displays all your alerts, from newest to oldest, each with a category icon, a title, a message and its age ("5 min ago", "2h ago", and so on). The page's subtitle always shows how many notifications are still unread, or "All caught up" when you are up to date. Unread notifications are highlighted (distinct background, bold title, colored dot). Clicking a notification marks it as read and, when it is linked to a specific item (an intervention, a payment, a reservation...), takes you directly to the relevant page.

## What types of notifications does Baitly send?

Notifications cover all the activity of your organization and are grouped into categories, which you find as filter tabs on the Notifications page: All, Unread, Interventions, Requests (service requests), Payments, Reservations, System, Contact and Documents. Concretely, you are alerted for example when: an intervention is assigned to you, starts, finishes or runs late; a service request is created, approved or urgent; a payment is confirmed, fails, or a payout is sent to you; an issue is reported on a property; a calendar import (iCal) succeeds or fails; a message or a contact form is received; a PDF document is generated or sent by email; a member joins your team or a role changes. Sensor alerts (noise) and system events (platform information) also arrive in this same center.

## How do I mark my notifications as read or delete them?

Three gestures are enough. One click on a notification marks it as read (and opens the related item if there is one). The "Mark all as read" button, shown at the top of the page whenever unread notifications remain, switches everything to read in one go. Finally, to delete a notification you no longer need, hover over its row: a delete icon appears on the right; click it to remove the notification from the list permanently. Use the "Unread" tab to see only what still needs your attention, and the category tabs (Interventions, Payments, Reservations...) to sort things out when the list is long. The list is paginated automatically based on your screen size.

## How do I choose which notifications I receive?

Go to Settings, Notifications tab. There you find your notification preferences, organized into collapsible sections by theme: Interventions, Service requests, Payments, Teams, iCal import, Wallets, Users, GDPR, Permissions, Properties, Contact & Messaging and Documents. Each specific event has its own switch (for example "Intervention completed", "Payment failed", "iCal import failed", "Message received"...), with a short description. A section switch turns a whole category on or off in one gesture, and a counter (for example "12/18") shows how many events are active in each section. Remember to save after your changes. Disabling a notification turns it off across all its delivery channels. This tab is also where marketing preferences (the Baitly newsletter) are managed, and they can be enabled or disabled at any time.

## Do I receive notifications by email or on mobile?

Yes, depending on the events and your profile. The main channel remains the web application's notification center (the bell). Some important events are additionally sent by email, for example the assignment of a mission to a worker, or the sending of a document. Field teams (technicians, housekeepers) who use the Baitly mobile app receive push notifications on their phone: a push notification linked to a mission opens that mission directly in the app. The preferences set in Settings, Notifications tab apply to your notifications whatever the channel: a disabled event type is no longer notified to you. Configuring notifications is also one of the suggested steps during the initial Baitly onboarding.

## Frequently asked questions

**How do I know if I have new notifications?**
A badge with the number of unread notifications appears on the bell icon in the sidebar. On the Notifications page, the subtitle repeats this counter and the "Unread" tab isolates what remains to be handled.

**I receive too many notifications, how do I reduce them?**
Open Settings, Notifications tab, and disable the events that do not interest you, individually or by whole section (section switch). Do not forget to save.

**Can a deleted notification be recovered?**
No, deletion is permanent. If you only want it out of your "to handle" queue, mark it as read rather than deleting it.

**Why is a member of my team not receiving a notification?**
Each user sets their own preferences: ask them to check in their Settings, Notifications tab, that the event in question is enabled. Notifications are also targeted: everyone only receives the events that concern them (their missions, their properties, their organization).

**Do push notifications work on the web?**
Web application notifications arrive in the notification center (the bell). Push notifications on the phone concern the Baitly mobile app used by field teams.
