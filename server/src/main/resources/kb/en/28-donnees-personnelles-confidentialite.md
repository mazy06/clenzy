# Personal data and privacy in Baitly

## What personal data does Baitly process?

Baitly processes two main families of data. On one side, the data of platform users (you and your teams): identity (last name, first name, email, phone), professional information (organization type, company, city), interface preferences and technical usage logs. Billing data goes through the payment provider: Baitly stores no card numbers. On the other side, guest data: the guest record contains last name, first name, email, phone, nationality, language and notes, as well as the stay history (number of reservations, channel, amounts). This information serves to manage reservations, client communication and local reporting obligations. Baitly's privacy policy, publicly accessible (notably from the registration form), details the data categories, purposes and recipients.

## Who can see personal data in Baitly?

Access is compartmentalized on two levels. First level: the organization. Each organization strictly sees only its own data (guests, reservations, finances); only the platform roles of the Baitly team (Super Admin, Super Manager) have cross-organization visibility, as part of administering the service. Second level: the role within the organization. A technician or a housekeeper only sees their missions, not the revenue or the guest database; the guest list requires the corresponding permission; user account management is reserved for administrators. The confidentiality rule applied to amounts: everyone only sees what concerns them (the provider sees their pay, the host the billed price). The details of roles and the fine-grained tuning of permissions module by module are described in the users, roles and permissions article.

## How is guest data used for the police record?

When local regulations require reporting hosted guests, the guest record feeds the police record: the reservation shows the primary guest and their companions, with a submission state ("Submitted" or "To submit" to the official service) and a "Resubmit" action in case of failure. Baitly collects only the information necessary for this legal obligation. The full workings (entering companions, submission statuses) are described in the article on reservations. A good habit: fill in the guest record accurately (identity, nationality), because this is the information that gets submitted; any correction is made on the record and then resubmitted.

## How do I manage my consents and the newsletter?

Your communication choices are set in Settings, Notifications tab. There you find on one side the activity notification preferences (events related to your properties, interventions, payments, which can be disabled individually or by section), and on the other side the marketing preferences: the subscription to the Baitly newsletter can be turned on and off at any time with a simple switch. Notifications about your activity (for example a failed payment or an assigned intervention) are distinct from marketing communication: turning off the newsletter does not turn off operational alerts, and vice versa. The privacy policy specifies the basis of each type of sending and reminds you that consent to the newsletter can be withdrawn at any time.

## How do I request the deletion or export of my data?

Baitly's privacy policy describes the rights provided by personal data regulations (access, rectification, erasure, restriction, portability, objection, withdrawal of consent) and the contact point for exercising them. The interface does not currently offer a self-service button to export or delete all your data: for an export, deletion or anonymization request (for your account, a departed member, or the data of a guest who requests it), contact Baitly support, which will handle the request through the internal procedures designed for this purpose. Note that some data must be kept for the legal retention periods (for example accounting records): deletion then covers what is not subject to a retention obligation.

## What are good practices to protect my organization's data?

A few simple habits eliminate most of the risk. Never share an account: each collaborator must have their own access, which also guarantees the traceability of actions. Apply least privilege: give each member the most restricted role that lets them do their job (a housekeeper does not need to see billing), and fine-tune if needed in Roles & Permissions. Remove members who leave your organization without delay (Settings, Organization tab). Do not share sensitive information through unintended channels: Baitly, for example, never sends property access codes by email. Finally, keep the free-text notes of guest records to information useful for the stay: avoid recording sensitive data that has no reason to be there.

## Frequently asked questions

**Can another manager see my guests?**
No. Data is compartmentalized by organization: each organization only sees its own guests, reservations and finances. Only the Baitly platform team has cross-organization visibility to administer the service.

**Does Baitly store card numbers?**
No. Payments go through the payment provider; Baitly keeps no card numbers.

**A guest asks me to delete their data, what should I do?**
Contact Baitly support with the request: deletion or anonymization is handled through the established procedures, taking legal retention periods into account (invoices in particular).

**Where can I unsubscribe from the newsletter?**
In Settings, Notifications tab, marketing preferences section: the newsletter switch can be turned off at any time.

**How do I limit what a member of my team can see?**
Give them the role suited to their job when inviting them, then fine-tune permissions module by module in Roles & Permissions (reserved for administrators).
