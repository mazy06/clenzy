# Dashboards, analytics and reports in Baitly

## What can be found on the dashboard?

The "Dashboard" is Baitly's home page. It is organized into tabs: Overview, Planning, Activity, Operations, Noise monitoring, Analytics, Simulator, and depending on your equipment, Smart locks and Key management. The overview displays key indicator (KPI) cards tailored to your role: a manager sees occupancy, revenue and the health of channel synchronization (Airbnb, Booking, etc.); a host sees their properties; a technician sees their interventions. A period filter (7 days, 30 days, 90 days, 1 year) applies to all statistics. The Activity tab lists recent events, the Operations tab tracks the field (cleanings, interventions), and the Planning tab gives a multi-property calendar view.

## What does the dashboard's Analytics tab contain?

The Analytics tab (visible to management roles: administrator, manager, supervisor) offers an in-depth analysis of portfolio performance in 11 sections. Overall performance: RevPAN (revenue per available night), ADR (average price per sold night), occupancy rate, total revenue, net margin, ROI and average length of stay — each indicator displays its trend versus the previous period. Revenue: revenue/expense evolution over 6 months, breakdown by channel (Airbnb, Booking, direct), top 5 properties. Occupancy: occupied/vacant by month, rate per property, vacant nights and a heatmap of the last 42 days. Pricing: average price vs RevPAN, price by property type, optimal price, price/occupancy elasticity. Added to these are forecasts (30, 90 and 365 days, three optimistic/realistic/pessimistic scenarios), recommendations quantified in euros and sorted by impact, a guest base analysis, a ranking of properties by performance score (0 to 100), a portfolio/best-property comparison, an interactive simulator (price and target occupancy sliders) and business alerts sorted by severity.

## How do I generate and download a report?

The "Reports" menu gathers the reports generated by Baitly, in four families: financial report, interventions report, teams report and properties report. Choose the report, the period (current, weekly, monthly, annual), click "Generate", then "Download" to get the PDF. The reports include charts: interventions by status, by type, by month, by priority, team performance, interventions per property, revenue by month, cost breakdown. Access to reports depends on a dedicated permission (administrators and managers by default): if a report type is denied to you, contact your administrator. A fiscal tab also provides a VAT summary by period (monthly, quarterly, annual) with a breakdown by rate, including in multi-currency.

## Which exports are available for my accounting?

Baitly offers several accounting and financial exports from the Accounting and Reports modules: CSV export of reservations, CSV export of owner payouts, and FEC export (the accounting entries file in the French tax administration's format), directly transmittable to your accountant. The PDF reports (financial, interventions, teams, properties) complement these exports for presentation. On the owner side, Baitly generates a monthly statement per owner (a summary of revenue, commissions and payouts) sent by email, and each owner can track their properties, revenue and payouts in a dedicated owner portal.

## How do I read the ADR, RevPAN and occupancy rate indicators?

The occupancy rate is the share of available nights actually occupied over the period (occupied nights divided by available nights). ADR (Average Daily Rate) is the average price of a sold night: total revenue divided by the number of occupied nights. RevPAN (revenue per available night) combines the two: total revenue divided by all the portfolio's available nights — it is the best overall indicator, because a high ADR with low occupancy or high occupancy at knockdown prices both yield a poor RevPAN. Net margin relates revenue minus intervention costs to total revenue. Each indicator is compared to the previous period of the same length to display an upward, downward or stable trend.

## Frequently asked questions

**Why can't I see the Analytics tab?**
It is reserved for management roles (administrator, manager, supervisor). The overview's indicator cards remain visible to all roles, with adapted metrics.

**Can I change the analyzed period?**
Yes: a 7-day / 30-day / 90-day / 1-year filter applies to the dashboard and the Analytics tab. Forecasts, however, always rely on the full recent history.

**How do I get an export for my accountant?**
Use the FEC export (the French tax administration's standard) and the CSV exports of reservations and payouts, available in the accounting section.

**Where do the Analytics tab's recommendations come from?**
From a rules engine that detects situations to correct (low occupancy, vacant nights, insufficient margin, underpriced rates...) and estimates the impact in euros with a confidence level.

**Can a property's owner see their figures?**
Yes, through the owner portal: a summary of their properties, revenue and payouts, complemented by the monthly statement sent by email.
