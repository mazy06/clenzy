# Field teams: technicians, housekeepers and supervisors

## How do I invite a technician, a housekeeper or a supervisor?

Baitly field team members are users of your organization, managed from the Directory (users and teams). A manager creates the collaborator's account or sends them an invitation: the invitee receives an email with an acceptance link and joins the organization with the assigned role. The available field roles are: Technician (maintenance, repairs), Housekeeper (cleaning), Supervisor (team supervision), as well as Laundry and Exterior Tech. Alongside these, the Host (owner) role manages their properties, and the platform roles Super Admin and Super Manager administer everything. Once their account is active, the field worker uses the Baitly mobile app to receive their missions, document them (photos, checklist, signature) and track their history.

## How do I create a team and add members to it?

Teams are managed in the Directory, Teams tab. A team groups field workers around a service type: at creation (the "New team" button), you enter the name (for example "City Center Cleaning Team"), a description, the intervention type covered (cleaning, maintenance, etc.), then you add the members. Only users whose role matches the intervention type are offered (housekeepers for a cleaning team, technicians for a maintenance team). Each member can receive a role internal to the team (a free label, for example lead or member). The same user can belong to several teams. A team can be assigned to a property: it then becomes the priority team for that property's interventions. A team's detail page also shows its workload: active interventions, completed this month, pending, and a capacity indicator (available, busy, overloaded). Roles involved: creating and modifying teams is the managers' responsibility.

## What are a team's coverage areas for?

Each team can declare one or more geographic coverage areas, defined by country, department, district and city. These areas feed the automatic assignment of interventions: when no team is directly assigned to the property concerned, Baitly looks for a team whose coverage area matches the property's location, whose service type matches the request (cleaning, maintenance...) and which is available on the slot. This is what allows several teams to rotate over a portfolio of properties spread across multiple cities without systematic manual assignment. Areas are configured in the team's form (Directory, Teams tab). Roles involved: managers for configuration; field workers have nothing to do, they receive their missions.

## How do I manage field workers' rates and services?

Each provider (housekeeper, technician) can have their own rates, framed by the platform's recommended scale. Two forms exist: a flat rate for a specific property (which takes priority) or a general hourly rate (the price becomes the property's standard duration multiplied by their rate and the cleaning type). The field worker manages this in Settings, "My rates" screen: they see their rate, their per-property flat rates, and for each one a benchmark against the recommendation (an "in the market" badge if the rate is within the range, otherwise the gap as a percentage) — informative, never blocking. They also see their quality score there (0 to 100 over a rolling 30 days, based on the rate of missions completed with a proof photo). The manager can view a member's rates and score from the user list ("Rates and score" dialog). Technicians also have a "My maintenance rates" screen for their maintenance services. When a provider is assigned to a cleaning, their rate automatically applies to the mission's amount, unless it has already been paid or validated.

## What does each role see in Baitly?

Visibility depends strictly on the role. A technician, a housekeeper, a supervisor (or laundry, exterior tech) only sees the interventions assigned to them directly or assigned to a team they belong to — never anyone else's. A host (owner) only sees the interventions and requests related to their own properties. Platform managers (super admin, super manager) see everything and are the only ones who can assign interventions. Special case: field workers of a shared platform service provider can receive missions from several client organizations; they then see the missions assigned to them regardless of the originating organization, but their teams and other data remain those of their own structure. In all cases, everyone sees the amounts that concern them: the provider sees their pay, the host and managers see the billed price.

## Frequently asked questions

**Can a housekeeper see their colleagues' missions?**
No. They only see the missions assigned to them personally or assigned to a team they belong to.

**Can the same field worker be in several teams?**
Yes. A user can belong to several teams, for example a cleaning team in their city and a team covering a neighboring area.

**How is a team chosen automatically for a cleaning?**
Baitly first takes the team assigned to the property; otherwise it searches by coverage area, service type and availability, with automatic retries every 15 minutes if no team is found.

**Is a field worker required to follow the platform's scale?**
No. The recommended scale is a benchmark: the field worker sets their hourly rate or flat rates freely, with a simple indicator of the gap from the recommendation.

**Who can create or modify a team?**
Platform managers (super admin, super manager). Hosts and field workers can view, but do not manage team composition.
