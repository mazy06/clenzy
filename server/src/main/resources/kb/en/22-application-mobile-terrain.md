# The Baitly mobile app for field teams

## What is the Baitly mobile app for?

The Baitly mobile app is built for field teams: housekeepers, maintenance technicians and team managers. It lets you view your missions for the day, open the detail of an intervention, take the before and after photos of the job, follow a checklist, report an issue, sign off the end of a mission and communicate with your manager. You sign in with the same credentials (email or username and password) as on the web application: the screen shown depends on your role. A housekeeper gets the Today, Messages, History and Profile tabs; a technician gets Tickets, Messages, History and Profile; a manager has views for planning, interventions, teams and task validation.

## How do I see my interventions of the day on mobile?

For a housekeeper, the "Today" tab lists the missions assigned to you. A banner with the days of the week lets you move from one day to another (Today, Tomorrow, then the following dates), and indicators summarize your week: total number of missions, completed, upcoming (including urgent ones), in progress, number of properties, estimated hours and progress rate. The list refreshes automatically and continuously, no need to reload. On the technician side, the "Tickets" tab lists maintenance interventions with filters by status (pending, in progress) and by priority. The History tab keeps past missions. Each mission shows its status: Pending, Scheduled, In progress, Completed, or Cancelled.

## What does the detail of a cleaning mission contain?

When you open a mission, you see the property name, the service type, the status, and the pay planned for the mission (with, for information, a comparison against the recommended rate). The flow is guided by a four-step timeline: Before photos, Cleaning, After photos, Signature. A work timer measures your time on site (it keeps running even if you leave the app for a moment). A checklist organized by room (entrance, living room, kitchen, bathroom, bedroom) helps you forget nothing; your checkmarks are kept on your phone. A "Contact manager" shortcut lets you reach your manager if you have a question. The detail may also mention the schedule, the estimated duration, whether the mission is urgent, and notes.

## How do I take the before and after photos (proof of cleaning)?

Photos are at the heart of the mission. At the start, take the "before" photos: the capture screen offers to use the camera or pick images from the gallery; at least one photo is required to submit. Sending the "before" photos automatically moves the mission to "In progress" (a "Start cleaning" button also exists). Once the job is done, take the "after" photos: they are the proof of cleaning. This matters for providers paid per mission: the payout is conditional on at least one saved "after" photo, and without it the payment stays blocked for "missing proof". These photos also feed your quality score. If there is no network, photos are queued and sent automatically as soon as the connection returns.

## How do I finish a mission and get paid?

To close the mission, use "Finish and sign": the signature screen records the end of the mission and moves the status to "Completed". For an independent provider to be paid, three conditions must be met: the mission is completed, at least one "after" photo has been sent (proof of cleaning), and the payout account is configured in the profile (the "My payouts" section, with a guided identity verification to receive payments securely). If a condition is missing, the payout is blocked with its reason, and a notification invites you to fix it (for example configuring your payout account); a manager can retry the payment once the point is resolved. When everything is in order, the payout goes out automatically and you receive a "Payout sent" notification. The "My payouts" section of the profile summarizes your payments; "My rates" shows your terms.

## What notifications does the mobile app send?

The app sends push notifications to keep you informed without having to open it: new mission assigned (to you or your team), start or end of an intervention, status change, payout sent or failed, payout blocked pending account setup, issue reported or converted, new message and updates to service requests. Tapping a notification opens the relevant screen directly (the intervention detail, your payouts, your reports). Remember to allow notifications the first time you open the app; they are automatically disabled for the device when you sign out. A bell in the header also flags unread items.

## Can I report a problem from the field?

Yes. During a cleaning mission, the issue reporting screen lets you flag a problem found in the property (damage, broken item, missing equipment, and so on) without interrupting your work. The report is sent to the manager, who can turn it into a maintenance service request. You can find your reports in the "My reports" section of the profile and receive a notification when one of them is handled or converted. Technicians, for their part, have a diagnostic form, a supporting-photos screen and an end-of-intervention report with signature.

## Frequently asked questions

**Do I need to create a special account for the mobile app?**
No, sign in with the same credentials as on the web application. The home screen adapts automatically to your role.

**What happens if I have no network during a mission?**
You can keep working: photos and actions are queued on the phone and synchronized automatically when the connection returns.

**Why is my payment blocked after a completed mission?**
Check three points: the mission is indeed "Completed", at least one "after" photo has been sent, and your payout account is configured in "My payouts". The blocking notification states the reason.

**Can I choose my missions in the app?**
No, missions are assigned to you by your manager or automatically. If you are unavailable, tell your manager, who can reassign the mission.
